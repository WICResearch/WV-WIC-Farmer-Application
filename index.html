Become an Authorized WV WIC Farmer

Apply to accept WIC Cash Value Benefits and Farmers Market Nutrition Program benefits. This application takes about 8-10 minutes to complete.

Before you begin: To receive authorization, you must have cellular service or Wi-Fi at your selling location and be willing to use a personal phone or mobile device to process electronic transactions.

Step 5

Farmer agreement

Review the responsibilities below. You can open or close each section.

Farmer, farmers' market, and roadside stand responsibilities
  1. Display authorized WIC and WVFMNP signage.
  2. Accept electronic WIC FMNP benefits during the June 1 through October 31 season.
  3. Accept WIC Cash Value Benefits year-round when open.
  4. Accept benefits only from eligible participants, caretakers, or proxies.
  5. Provide eligible fruits and vegetables at the same quality and price offered to other customers.
  6. Process benefits only for eligible foods.
  7. Keep WIC and FMNP shopper information confidential.
  8. Complete required training and train employees.
  9. Provide records and reports requested by the program.
  10. Allow overt and covert compliance monitoring.
  11. Be responsible for employee actions.
  12. Repay benefits processed in violation of the agreement.
  13. Treat WIC shoppers with the same courtesy as all other customers.
  14. Follow USDA nondiscrimination requirements.
  15. Notify WV WIC about closure, address changes, or corrections.
  16. Grow at least one fruit or vegetable offered for sale.
  17. Sell fruits and vegetables grown in West Virginia or within 50 miles of the state border.
  18. Do not collect sales tax on eligible purchases.
  19. Do not require a participant to use the full benefit amount.
  20. Do not seek payment from participants for unpaid or partially paid benefits.
  21. Do not provide cash change, rain checks, or IOUs.
  22. Do not traffic benefits.
  23. Report suspected participant fraud or violations.
  24. Follow signature requirements for paper checks; electronic benefits do not require signatures.
  25. Follow all WV WIC operating rules, standards, and technical requirements.
West Virginia WIC Program responsibilities
  1. Accept and review applications fairly while supporting statewide access.
  2. Authorize eligible farmers, farmers' markets, and roadside stands.
  3. Use consistent participation standards without discrimination.
  4. Issue electronic benefits to eligible participants.
  5. Maintain sufficient funds for authorized banking activity.
  6. Provide signs, food lists, handbooks, and training materials.
  7. Provide training and guidance at required intervals.
  8. Provide technical assistance and compliance monitoring.
  9. Notify farmers of policy or procedure changes.
  10. Maintain a fair hearing process for appealable adverse actions.
General terms
  • The agreement runs from the final signature date through March 31, 2029.
  • Either party may terminate for cause with required notice.
  • Improperly redeemed transactions may be denied or recovered.
  • Fraud or program abuse may lead to disqualification or prosecution.
  • Applicants may appeal certain denials, sanctions, or disqualifications.
  • No conflict of interest may exist.
  • The agreement does not authorize the sale of foods other than eligible fruits and vegetables.
  • Noncompliance may result in claims or sanctions.

Step 6

Civil rights assurance

Authorized farmers must comply with applicable federal and state civil rights laws and may not discriminate in WIC or FMNP activities.

Read the full civil rights assurance

The applicant agrees to comply with Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, the Age Discrimination Act of 1975, Titles II and III of the Americans with Disabilities Act, Executive Order 13166, USDA civil rights regulations, and West Virginia Department of Health directives.

No person may be excluded from participation in, denied the benefits of, or subjected to discrimination under a covered program because of race, color, national origin, age, sex, disability, or protected prior civil rights activity.

Persons who need alternative communication methods may contact the administering agency or use Telecommunications Relay Service 711. Program information may also be made available in languages other than English.

To request the USDA Program Discrimination Complaint Form, call 866-632-9992. Complaint instructions are available from USDA.

USDA is an equal opportunity provider, employer, and lender.
